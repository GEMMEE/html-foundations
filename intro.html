You might expect that the paragraph I am writing right now is going to be displayed as two paragraphs because of the fact that I am going to separate them by spaces like this. However that is not the case, as you might see by running it. It ignores the spaces. When the browser encounters new lines like this in your HTML, it will compress them down into one single space. The result of this compression is that all of the text is clumped together into one long line.

If you want to create paragraphs in HTML, we need to use the paragraph element like in this case, which will add a newline after each of our paragraphs. A paragragh element is defined by wrapping text content with a p tag.

Headings are different from other HTML text elements: they are displayed larger and bolder than other to signify that they are headings. There are 6 different levels of headings starting from h1 to h6. The largest and most important heading is h1, while h6 is the tiniest heading at the lowest level. Let's see them in action.

This is a heading 2

This is a heading 4

This is a heading 5
This is a heading 6

Using the correct level of heading is important as levels provide a hierarchy to the content. An h1 heading should always be used for the heading of the overall page, and the lower level headings should be used as the headings for content in smaller sections of the page.

The em element makes text italic. It also semantically places emphasis on the text, which again may affect things like screen readers.

I have an emphasized word. To make the word emphasized italic as in previous sententence we wrap the word in a em tag.

I am an emphasized element and you can use me on my own too.
